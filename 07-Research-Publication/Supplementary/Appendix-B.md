# Appendix B

# Research Reproducibility and Artifact Verification

## Overview

This appendix documents the reproducibility methodology supporting the HyperSync research program.

The objective is to enable independent reviewers, researchers, and future collaborators to verify the relationship between the architectural concepts presented throughout the manuscript and the implementation artifacts contained within the HyperSync Ecosystem repository.

Rather than reproducing benchmark results alone, HyperSync emphasizes architectural reproducibility through implementation traceability, governance documentation, operational evidence, and version-controlled engineering artifacts.

---

# Reproducibility Objectives

The HyperSync research program seeks to ensure that readers can:

- Verify architectural claims.
- Inspect implementation artifacts.
- Review governance mechanisms.
- Examine operational documentation.
- Follow implementation evolution.
- Validate architectural consistency.

# Verification Process

Independent reviewers may verify the architecture by following the sequence below:

1. Read the manuscript.
2. Review the repository architecture.
3. Inspect the Operating System documentation.
4. Review governance documentation.
5. Examine implementation evidence.
6. Compare architectural claims with supporting artifacts.
7. Verify implementation consistency across the ecosystem.

---

# Evidence-Oriented Engineering

HyperSync adopts an evidence-oriented engineering methodology in which:

- Architectural claims are documented.
- Implementation artifacts are preserved.
- Governance decisions are recorded.
- Operational evidence is maintained.
- Research evolution is version controlled.

This methodology supports long-term architectural evolution while preserving reproducibility and reviewer confidence.
